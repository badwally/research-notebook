## Abstract

Sugar addiction has been proposed as a behavioural syndrome characterised by craving, loss of control, escalation of intake, and continued use despite adverse consequences. Although not formally recognised in current diagnostic systems, converging evidence suggests that addictive-like responding is most plausible for refined, rapidly delivered sugar vehicles (e.g., sugar-sweetened beverages) in vulnerable individuals, and overlaps substantially with loss-of-control eating presentations, including binge eating disorder (BED).

We conducted a narrative review of PubMed, Scopus, and Web of Science (June-September 2025) using sugar- and mechanism-oriented terms (including sucrose, glucose-fructose, and high-fructose corn syrup), and integrated preclinical, neuroimaging, clinical, and policy evidence within a translational framework.
